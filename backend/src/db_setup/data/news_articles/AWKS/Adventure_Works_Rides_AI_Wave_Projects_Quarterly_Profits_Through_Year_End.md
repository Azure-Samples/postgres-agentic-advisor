Adventure Works Rides AI Wave, Projects Quarterly Profits Through Year-End
A burst of interest in artificial intelligence is reshaping the technology landscape, and Adventure Works
Analytics is leaning heavily into the surge. On Monday, the company announced it anticipates remaining
profitable for every quarter of 2023, a milestone driven by its burgeoning new Enterprise Ai Platform

bedrock of its performance, generating $488.41 million in revenue, up 23% year-over-year, while
commercial revenue climbed 17% to $398.84 million.
The announcement drove Adventure Works's shares up by roughly 23% in after-hours trading, as investors
digested the prospect of sustained profitability and the potential implications of the company's aggressive
pivot into AI.
Adventure Works Core Intelligence, which blends Adventure Works's established machine learning
capabilities with cutting-edge large language models similar to those powering StudyBot AI, is set to launch
this month for select customers. Early adopters include multinational insurers, supply chain managers, and
defense organizations. The platform is designed to process complex natural language queries while
maintaining stringent controls over data access and privacy, aligning with regulatory and ethical standards.
"Our architecture and organization have been preparing for exactly this paradigm shift," CEO Nathan
Reeves wrote in a letter. He highlighted the platform's ability to integrate public and proprietary datasets,
which he believes will unlock novel use cases across industries such as aviation, energy, pharmaceuticals,
and automotive manufacturing.
Though the company reported positive operating income for the first time--a $6.76 million margin, or
4%--some challenges remain. Second-quarter revenue forecasts of $892.32 million to $899.08 million fell
short of the $906.18 million consensus estimate, and CFO Owen Whitfield acknowledged internationally
uneven demand. Nonetheless, U.S. performance remained a bright spot, with U.S. commercial revenue
leaping 28% year-over-year, reflecting greater traction among domestic businesses.
Adventure Works has also tightened costs, including a 4% workforce reduction earlier this year, to sharpen
its focus on AI developments. The company expects full-year 2023 revenue of $3.69 billion to $3.78 billion
and pledged Accounting Standards profitability in each remaining quarter.
While some analysts, like Sterling Financial Group's Morgan Ellis, expressed optimism about the AI
initiative's prospects, others noted the importance of delivering on customer uptake and scaling the nascent
Adventure Works Core Intelligence. For now, Adventure Works is positioning itself as a key player riding
the generative AI tide, betting that its new technologies can translate into sustained financial gains.
