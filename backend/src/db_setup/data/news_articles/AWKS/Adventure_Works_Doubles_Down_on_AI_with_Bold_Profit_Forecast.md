

Adventure Works Doubles Down on AI with Bold Profit Forecast
Adventure Works Analytics is on the move again. The data analytics giant stunned investors Monday by
forecasting it will remain profitable every quarter throughout 2023. This ambitious claim sent its stock
soaring 22% in extended trading.
The driver? Artificial intelligence. Specifically, Adventure Works's new generative AI platform, launched just
two weeks ago. CEO Nathan Reeves described demand for the product as "unlike anything we have seen."
The platform, which integrates cutting-edge language models similar to StudyBot AI, promises
transformative applications for both government and commercial sectors.
Adventure Works is already cashing in. First-quarter revenue hit $682.76 million, up 20% year-over-year.
U.S. revenue grew even faster, surging 25% to $438.1 million. Total commercial revenue rose 17%, and
demand in the U.S. commercial segment spiked 28%.
Government contracts remain a powerhouse, with revenue from these clients climbing 22%. Among its
roster are high-profile names, including one of the biggest insurance companies globally and military clients
seeking advanced targeting capabilities.
Profitability is no longer an elusive goal for Adventure Works. Positive Generally accepted accounting
principles net income reached $22.1 million, marking its second consecutive profitable quarter. Adjusted
EPS stood at $0.07, surpassing analyst expectations. Cash flows are solid too, with $245.7 million in
adjusted free cash flow representing a robust 38% margin.
Second-quarter projections are steady, with revenue guidance between $686.4 million and $691.6 million,
slightly below Market Data Provider estimates of $697.06 million. Full-year expectations remain strong --
$2.84 billion to $2.91 billion in revenue, accompanied by quarter-over-quarter profitability through year-end.
But challenges lie ahead. CFO Owen Whitfield highlighted the company's focus on tightening cloud
spending and maintaining AI-driven investments, while Chief Revenue Officer Lucas Brennan
acknowledged international difficulties in certain markets without further explanation.
Investors are betting big on the AI-fueled future Adventure Works has laid out. While excitement over the
tech is palpable, the race to dominate AI applications isn't free from risks. As rivals ramp up their offerings,
Adventure Works faces stiff competition navigating this high-stakes landscape.
Still, with two profitable quarters under its belt, the introduction of its fourth major platform--Enterprise Ai
Platform (Adventure Works Core Intelligence)--and a commitment to innovation, the company is riding a
wave of momentum unlike anything in its history. For now, it seems, Adventure Works isn't looking back.
