

Contoso Compute Corp Reports Record Revenue and Earnings in Q3 Fiscal
Contoso Compute Reports Historic Q3 Fiscal 2024 Results, Driven by Data Center Boom
## Record Revenue Up 150% From Last Year, Data Center Revenue Surges
Contoso Compute Corporation has unveiled its financial results for the third quarter of fiscal year 2024,
posting unprecedented growth fueled largely by its Data Center business. For the quarter ending
September 29, 2023, the tech giant reported record-breaking revenue of $23.56 billion, reflecting a
remarkable 150% jump compared to the same period a year ago and a sequential increase from Q2 FY24.
## Key Financial Highlights
The standout growth driver for Contoso Compute this past quarter was its Data Center segment, which
accounted for $18.86 billion in revenue, up significantly year-over-year. This division's performance
underscores the heightened demand generated by advancements in AI and performance-optimized
processing technologies.
Gross margin improvements bolstered the company's profitability as it climbed to 76.0% on an Accounting
Standards basis, showing a strong increase from Q3 FY23, while non-Accounting Standards gross margins
reached 77.0%, representing a substantial margin expansion.
Operating income also soared to new heights. On an Accounting Standards basis, Contoso Compute
recorded $13.55 billion in operating income, compared to just $781.3 million in the same quarter last year.
Non-Accounting Standards operating income hit $15.03 billion, further demonstrating the company's ability
to capitalize on growing demand.
Net income reflected similar staggering growth. Contoso Compute's overall Accounting Standards net
income for Q3 surged to $12.01 billion--marking a tremendous year-over-year increase. On a
non-Accounting Standards basis, net income reached $13.03 billion, up significantly compared to the prior
year. Diluted earnings per share followed the same trajectory, with Accounting Standards EPS climbing to
$4.82, a sharp rise from $0.35, and non-Accounting Standards EPS reaching $5.23.
Strong Balance Sheet and Cash Flow
As of September 29, Contoso Compute maintained a strong financial position, with $23.76 billion in cash,
cash equivalents, and marketable securities. Total assets have grown to $70.39 billion, compared to $53.53
billion at the end of the previous fiscal year.
Operational cash flow contributed significantly to liquidity, generating $9.53 billion during the quarter.
Meanwhile, Contoso Compute kept operating expenses under effective control. Compared to the previous
quarter, operating expenses rose only modestly on both an Accounting Standards basis and a
non-Accounting Standards basis, a testament to the company's ability to scale efficiently despite surging
revenue.
Q4 FY24 Estimates and Forward Guidance
Looking ahead, Contoso Compute projects Q4 revenue to reach approximately $26 billion, with an
allowable variance of 4%. The gross margin outlook remains optimistic, with Accounting Standards gross
margins expected around 74.7% and non-Accounting Standards gross margins estimated at 75.7%.

Leadership Statements and Strategic Focus
Commenting on the robust quarterly performance, Contoso Compute's founder and CEO Daniel Reeves
said, "Our exponential growth reflects a paradigm shift across industries--from traditional legacy
infrastructure toward specialized, AI-accelerated compute. This is the dawn of a new era of AI."
Reeves went on to emphasize the strategic significance of Contoso Compute's cutting-edge solutions,
including its processing accelerator, high-velocity networking technology, AI Platform Suite software, and AI
Solutions Studio services--all instrumental in fueling the company's growth trajectory.
The optimism at Contoso Compute was echoed by the company's executive vice president and CFO,
Patricia Warren, in her detailed analysis of Contoso Compute's ongoing financial strategy and
market-leading infrastructure.
