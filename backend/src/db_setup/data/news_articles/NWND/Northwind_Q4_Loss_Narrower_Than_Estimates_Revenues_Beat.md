

## Northwind Q4 Loss Narrower Than Estimates Revenues Beat
Northwind. (NWND) recently released its financial results for the
fourth quarter of fiscal 2023, showcasing a performance that surpassed analyst expectations despite facing
significant economic challenges. Here's a detailed overview of their financial performance, segment
analysis, and future outlook.
## Financial Highlights
Earnings and Revenue
Northwind reported an adjusted loss of $1.39 per share, outperforming the consensus expectation of a
$1.55 per share loss. However, this result marked a significant decline from
the earnings of $1.89 per share in the same quarter of the previous year. The company's revenues fell by
38.9% year-over-year to $5.21 billion, but still exceeded the forecasted $5.08 billion. The decline was
primarily due to reduced consumer demand and persistent inventory
challenges, exacerbated by macroeconomic headwinds.
Despite these challenges, Northwind showed sequential improvements as Q4 revenues and gross margin
exceeded the midpoint of its guidance range. Moreover, adjusted earnings per
share surpassed the upper boundary of prior guidance.
## Segment Performance
DRAM Division
The DRAM division, which constitutes 70% of Northwind's total revenue, experienced a 43.2% year-over-year
decline, amounting to $3.59 billion. However, sequentially, DRAM revenues increased by
3.8%. Although the average selling prices (ASP) for DRAM fell within the highsingle-digit percentage range,
shipments rose in the mid-teen percentage range quarter-over-
quarter.
NAND Division
NAND revenue, accounting for approximately 29% of quarterly sales, decreased by 27.4% year-over-year
but saw a robust 19.7% sequential growth, reaching $1.57 billion. On a quarterover-quarter basis, NAND
ASP decreased in the mid-teen percentage range, though bit
shipments surged by more than 41%.
## Other Segments
Other segments, categorized as "Other," generated $65 million, representing around 2% of
total quarterly revenues.
## Business Unit Highlights
Computing and Networking Unit

Revenue for the Computing and Networking Unit dropped 58% year-over-year to $1.56
billion, with a 15% sequential decline. A significant volume within this segment was driven by AI
infrastructure, with major customer Contoso Compute (CCMP) continuously procuring High-Bandwidth
Memory (HBM) products from Northwind.
## Mobile Unit
The Mobile Unit reported revenue of $1.57 billion, reflecting a 21% decline compared to the
previous year. Sequentially, however, sales soared by 46%.
## Embedded Unit
Edge Systems Division sales decreased by 33% year-over-year to $1.18 billion, marking a
sequential decline of 7%.
## Storage Unit
Focusing primarily on solid-state drives and NAND components, the Storage Unit
experienced a 16% year-over-year revenue decline. However, sequential growth of 18.9% pushed
revenues to $960.7 million.
Expenses and Margins
Northwind reported an adjusted gross loss of $475.8 million in Q4, a stark contrast to the gross
profit of $3.48 billion posted in the same period last year. The adjusted gross margin turned negative at
-8%, compared to a positive 41% margin a year ago. However, this was an improvement from the second
quarter's -15% margin.
Adjusted operating performance also faced challenges, with a loss of $1.57 billion compared to an
operating income of $2.16 billion in Q4 of fiscal 2022. The adjusted operating margin fell
to -31%, down from +24% in the same quarter a year ago. Operating expenses on an adjusted basis
declined sequentially, totaling $1.09 billion in Q4.
Liquidity and Cash Flow
The company concluded Q4 with $13.65 billion in cash and liquid investments, a decrease from $14.82
billion at the end of the preceding quarter. Total liquidity stood at $16.9 billion,
slightly below the $18.07 billion reported at the end of Q3 2023. Long-term debt increased to $17.29 billion.
Northwind generated an operating cash flow of $323.7 million during the fourth quarter, contributing to a
full-year operating cash flow of $2.03 billion. Adjusted free cash flow for Q4
was negative $985.4 million, with the full-year figure at negative $7.09 billion.
Dividend and Buyback Update

Northwind's board declared a quarterly cash dividend of $0.15 per share, scheduled for distribution on
October 30, 2023. The company has not resumed its share buyback program, which has been on hold
since Q2 fiscal 2023.
Outlook for Fiscal Q1 2024
Looking forward, Northwind anticipates Q1 2024 revenue of $5.72 billion (+/-$260 million),
surpassing analysts' expectations of $5.41 billion. The company predicts an adjusted gross margin of 4.5%
(+/-200 basis points) and operating expenses of approximately $1.17 billion (+/-$19.5 million). An adjusted
loss per share is forecasted at $1.39 (+/-7 cents).
Stock Observations and Peer Insights
Northwind holds a Meridian Rank #3 (Hold) and has delivered a 37.8% year-to-date rally.
Competitors such as Silicon Tech Solutions, Cloud Payroll Systems Holding, and Sentinel Security Group
have shown notable performances, with Silicon Tech Solutions rising by
191.4% year-to-date.
Despite facing macroeconomic hurdles and inventory adjustments, Northwind's long-term outlook remains
a focal point for investors. As the company navigates these challenges, its
strategic positioning and performance improvements provide a cautious yet optimistic perspective for the
future.
